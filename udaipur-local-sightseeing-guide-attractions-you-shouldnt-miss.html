Udaipur Local Sightseeing Guide

Attractions You
Shouldn't Miss

Known as the City of Lakes, Udaipur is one of Rajasthan's most captivating destinations. Surrounded by the Aravalli Hills and steeped in centuries of Mewar history, the city offers a remarkable blend of royal architecture, serene lakes, cultural experiences, and vibrant local life.

10 Must-See
Attractions
Why Udaipur

Why Udaipur is Perfect
for Sightseeing

Unlike many cities where attractions are spread across large distances, most of Udaipur's major landmarks are relatively close to one another. The city's compact layout makes sightseeing both convenient and enjoyable.


Visitors can comfortably explore historic palaces, scenic lakes, ancient temples, heritage havelis, gardens, cultural venues, and local markets — all within easy reach of one another. Whether you're visiting for a weekend getaway, a family vacation, a romantic holiday, or a heritage-focused trip, Udaipur's attractions offer something for every traveler.

Local Sightseeing

Udaipur Attractions You Shouldn't Miss

This local sightseeing guide covers the places you simply shouldn't miss during your trip to Udaipur.

01
Landmark 01

City Palace: The Crown Jewel of Udaipur

Built over nearly four centuries, the palace is the largest royal complex in Rajasthan and offers an extraordinary glimpse into the history of the Mewar dynasty. Highlights include Mor Chowk, Crystal Gallery, Badi Mahal, royal courtyards, and lake-view terraces. The palace also provides some of the finest panoramic views of Lake Pichola and the Old City.

Recommended Time: 2–3 hours
02
Landmark 02

Lake Pichola

Constructed in the 14th century, Lake Pichola remains the heart of the city and is famous for its scenic beauty. Take a boat ride, visit Jag Mandir, watch the sunset, explore nearby ghats, and enjoy lakeside dining. The lake is particularly beautiful during sunrise and sunset.

Best Time to Visit: Sunrise and sunset
03
Landmark 03

Jagdish Temple

Located near City Palace, Jagdish Temple is one of the city's most important religious landmarks. Built in 1651, the temple is admired for its intricate carvings, historic architecture, and spiritual significance. Visitors interested in history and architecture often find it one of Udaipur's most rewarding attractions.

Built: 1651 — one of the city's most important religious landmarks
04
Landmark 04

Fateh Sagar Lake

For a more relaxed atmosphere, head to Fateh Sagar Lake. Surrounded by hills, the lake is popular for morning walks, evening drives, boating, photography, and local street food. It's also one of the favorite gathering spots for local residents.

Popular For: Morning walks, boating, photography, and local street food
05
Landmark 05

Bagore Ki Haveli

Situated on the waterfront at Gangaur Ghat, Bagore Ki Haveli offers both historical exhibits and cultural entertainment. Don't miss the evening cultural show featuring folk dances, puppet performances, and traditional music. This experience provides a deeper understanding of Rajasthan's artistic heritage.

Don't Miss: The evening cultural show with folk dances, puppet performances, and traditional music
06
Landmark 06

Sajjangarh Monsoon Palace

Perched high above the city, Sajjangarh Monsoon Palace offers breathtaking views of Lake Pichola, Fateh Sagar Lake, the Aravalli Hills, and the Udaipur skyline. Many travelers consider it the best sunset viewpoint in Udaipur.

Best For: The best sunset viewpoint in Udaipur
07
Landmark 07

Saheliyon Ki Bari

One of the city's most elegant attractions, Saheliyon Ki Bari was designed as a royal garden for princesses and their companions. Highlights include marble fountains, lotus ponds, decorative pavilions, and landscaped gardens. It's a peaceful stop during a busy sightseeing day.

Highlights: Marble fountains, lotus ponds, and decorative pavilions
08
Landmark 08

Gangaur Ghat

Located along Lake Pichola, Gangaur Ghat is one of the most photogenic locations in the city. Visitors often come here to relax by the water, watch local life, take photographs, and enjoy lake views. The atmosphere becomes especially magical during the evening hours.

Best Time: Evening hours, when the atmosphere becomes especially magical
09
Landmark 09

Shilpgram

For travelers interested in Rajasthan's rural culture, Shilpgram is worth visiting. The cultural complex showcases traditional architecture, handicrafts, artisan workshops, and folk performances. It's particularly popular among families and international visitors.

Popular With: Families and international visitors
10
Landmark 10

Local Markets of Udaipur

Exploring Udaipur's markets is a sightseeing experience in itself. Hathipole Bazaar is known for miniature paintings, traditional handicrafts, and textiles. Bapu Bazaar is popular for souvenirs, local clothing, and decorative items. These markets offer a glimpse into everyday life in Udaipur.

Key Markets: Hathipole Bazaar and Bapu Bazaar

Suggested Route

Suggested One-Day Udaipur Sightseeing Route

If you're short on time, this itinerary covers the city's most iconic attractions in a single day.

Morning — Stop 1

City Palace

Built over nearly four centuries, City Palace is the largest royal complex in Rajasthan and offers an extraordinary glimpse into the history of the Mewar dynasty.

  • Mor Chowk and Crystal Gallery
  • Badi Mahal and royal courtyards
  • Panoramic views of Lake Pichola and the Old City
  • Recommended Time: 2–3 hours

Morning — Stop 2

Jagdish Temple

Located near City Palace, Jagdish Temple is one of the city's most important religious landmarks. Built in 1651, the temple is admired for its intricate carvings, historic architecture, and spiritual significance.

  • Intricate carvings and historic architecture
  • Spiritual significance

Morning — Stop 3

Old City Walk

Explore the historic lanes surrounding City Palace, filled with artisan workshops, heritage havelis, local cafés, handicraft stores, and hidden temples.

  • Artisan workshops and heritage havelis
  • Local cafés and handicraft stores
  • Hidden temples

Afternoon — Stop 1

Lunch with Lake Views

Udaipur is famous for rooftop restaurants and lakeside dining spots. A meal with views of the shimmering lake is the perfect way to recharge for the afternoon ahead.

  • Lakeside dining
  • Rooftop restaurant options

Afternoon — Stop 2

Fateh Sagar Lake

Surrounded by hills, Fateh Sagar Lake is popular for its relaxed atmosphere. It's one of the favorite gathering spots for local residents.

  • Boating and photography
  • Local street food
  • Scenic views of the surrounding hills

Afternoon — Stop 3

Saheliyon Ki Bari

One of the city's most elegant attractions, Saheliyon Ki Bari was designed as a royal garden for princesses and their companions. It's a peaceful stop during a busy sightseeing day.

  • Marble fountains and lotus ponds
  • Decorative pavilions and landscaped gardens

Evening — Stop 1

Boat Ride on Lake Pichola

Lake Pichola is undoubtedly one of Udaipur's most iconic attractions. A boat ride on the lake offers beautiful views of Lake Palace, Jag Mandir, City Palace, and historic ghats.

  • Visit Jag Mandir
  • Views of Lake Palace and City Palace
  • Explore nearby ghats

Evening — Stop 2

Bagore Ki Haveli Show

Situated on the waterfront at Gangaur Ghat, Bagore Ki Haveli offers both historical exhibits and cultural entertainment. The evening show provides a deeper understanding of Rajasthan's artistic heritage.

  • Folk dances and puppet performances
  • Traditional music

Evening — Stop 3

Sunset at Gangaur Ghat

Located along Lake Pichola, Gangaur Ghat is one of the most photogenic locations in the city. The atmosphere becomes especially magical during the evening hours.

  • Relax by the water
  • Watch local life
  • Take photographs and enjoy lake views
Travel Tips

Tips for Local Sightseeing in Udaipur

Udaipur is much more than a collection of famous monuments. Its charm lies in the way lakes, palaces, culture, and everyday life blend together to create a truly memorable destination.


From the grandeur of City Palace and the tranquility of Lake Pichola to cultural performances and hidden heritage gems, Udaipur offers a sightseeing experience that feels both enriching and deeply personal.

Start Early

Most attractions are more enjoyable before the afternoon crowds arrive.

Wear Comfortable Footwear

Many historic areas are best explored on foot.

Carry Water

Particularly during warmer months.

Plan Sunset Experiences

Lake Pichola and Monsoon Palace offer some of the city's best sunset views.

Leave Time for Exploration

Some of Udaipur's most memorable moments happen between the major attractions.

Best Time to Visit

October to March offers the most pleasant weather for sightseeing.
