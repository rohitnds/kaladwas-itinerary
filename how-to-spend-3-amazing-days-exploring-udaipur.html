Udaipur Travel Guide

How to Spend 3 Amazing Days
Exploring Udaipur

Known as the City of Lakes, Udaipur is one of Rajasthan's most enchanting destinations. With its royal palaces, tranquil lakes, historic havelis, vibrant markets, and rich cultural heritage, the city offers far more than can be experienced in a single day.

3 Perfect
Days
Why Visit for Three Days

Why 3 Days is the Ideal Amount
of Time for Udaipur

A two-day trip covers the major attractions, but a three-day stay allows you to explore beyond the main tourist sites, enjoy leisurely lakefront experiences, discover local markets and cafés, experience Rajasthan's culture and cuisine, avoid rushing between attractions, and take day trips or enjoy scenic viewpoints.


Three days provide the perfect balance between sightseeing and relaxation.

Day 1

Discover Udaipur's Royal Heritage

Start your journey at the magnificent City Palace, the largest palace complex in Rajasthan. Built over centuries by the rulers of Mewar, the palace showcases ornate balconies, colorful courtyards, intricate mosaics, and breathtaking views of the city. Allow 2–3 hours to fully appreciate the palace.


A short walk from City Palace brings you to Jagdish Temple. This beautifully carved 17th-century temple is one of Udaipur's most important religious landmarks and offers a fascinating glimpse into Rajasthan's architectural heritage.

Late Morning

Visit Jagdish Temple

A short walk from City Palace

This beautifully carved 17th-century temple is one of Udaipur's most important religious landmarks and offers a fascinating glimpse into Rajasthan's architectural heritage.

Afternoon

Wander Through the Old City

Best explored on foot

The historic streets surrounding City Palace are among the most charming areas of Udaipur. Spend time exploring traditional artisan workshops, miniature painting studios, local boutiques, heritage havelis, and hidden cafés.

Evening

Sunset Boat Ride on Lake Pichola

No trip to Udaipur is complete without this

A sunset boat ride offers unforgettable views of City Palace, Jag Mandir, Lake Palace, and historic ghats. As the sun sets behind the Aravalli Hills, the lake becomes one of the most beautiful places in Rajasthan.

Day 2: Culture, Gardens and Local Experiences

8:00
AM
Fateh Sagar Lake

Morning

Visit Fateh Sagar Lake

A local favourite for morning walks

Compared to Lake Pichola, Fateh Sagar has a more local atmosphere and is a favorite spot for morning walks. Enjoy lake views, fresh morning air, local food stalls, and scenic drives.

Mid-Morning

Explore Saheliyon Ki Bari

Built for royal women

This historic garden remains one of Udaipur's most peaceful attractions. Highlights include lotus pools, marble fountains, beautiful landscaping, and royal architecture. It's an ideal escape from the city's busier areas.

Afternoon

Discover Local Markets

Hathipole · Bapu Bazaar · Clock Tower Market

Shopping is an essential part of any Udaipur itinerary. Look for handmade textiles, traditional jewelry, leather goods, miniature paintings, and local handicrafts.

Evening

Experience Bagore Ki Haveli

Gangaur Ghat

Located at Gangaur Ghat, Bagore Ki Haveli offers one of the city's most authentic cultural experiences. The evening cultural show features folk dances, puppet performances, traditional music, and Rajasthan's artistic heritage. This is one of the best ways to connect with local culture.

Day 3: Scenic Views and Hidden Gems

9:00
AM
Monsoon Palace

Morning

Visit Sajjangarh Monsoon Palace

The drive itself is scenic and rewarding

Perched high above the city, Sajjangarh Monsoon Palace offers panoramic views of Udaipur's lakes and surrounding hills. Originally built as a monsoon retreat, it is now one of the city's most popular viewpoints.

Mid-Day

Explore a Lesser-Known Side of Udaipur

Beyond the main tourist circuit

After visiting Monsoon Palace, spend some time exploring quieter attractions such as Ahar Cenotaphs, Vintage Car Museum, Shilpgram, and local art galleries. These places offer a different perspective on the city.

Afternoon

Enjoy Udaipur's Culinary Scene

Many rooftop restaurants with lake views

Udaipur is not just about sightseeing. Take time to sample local dishes such as Dal Baati Churma, Gatte Ki Sabzi, Ker Sangri, and Laal Maas.

Evening

Relax by the Lake

This slower pace is often what travelers remember most

Spend your final evening enjoying the relaxed atmosphere that makes Udaipur so special — a lakeside walk, café hopping in the Old City, sunset photography, or shopping for souvenirs.
