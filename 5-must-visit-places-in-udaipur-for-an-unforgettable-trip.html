5 Must-Visit Places in Udaipur
for an Unforgettable Trip

Known as the City of Lakes, Udaipur blends royal heritage, scenic beauty, vibrant culture, and timeless architecture among the Aravalli Hills.

5Iconic
Places
Why Visit Udaipur

One of India's Most
Loved Destinations

Founded in 1559 by Maharana Udai Singh II, Udaipur served as the historic capital of the Mewar Kingdom. What makes Udaipur unique is its ability to offer history, culture, architecture, food, shopping, and natural beauty within a compact and walkable setting.


Visitors can spend their mornings exploring royal palaces, afternoons wandering through historic lanes, and evenings enjoying sunset views over tranquil lakes. If you're planning your first trip to Udaipur, these five must-visit places deserve a place at the top of your itinerary.

The Essentials

Five Places You Cannot Miss

Each of these landmarks tells a chapter of Udaipur's story — from royal grandeur to spiritual calm, scenic waterways to living cultural tradition.

01
Landmark 01

City Palace: The Grand Symbol of Mewar Heritage

Standing majestically above Lake Pichola, City Palace is undoubtedly one of the most important places to visit in Udaipur. Built over nearly four centuries by successive rulers of Mewar, the palace complex showcases a remarkable blend of Rajput and Mughal architectural influences. Intricate balconies, ornate courtyards, colorful glasswork, marble structures, and panoramic viewpoints make it a highlight of any Udaipur itinerary.

02
Landmark 02

Lake Pichola: The Heart and Soul of Udaipur

Few experiences define a trip to Udaipur quite like a boat ride on Lake Pichola. Created in the 14th century, this artificial freshwater lake has become one of the city's most recognizable landmarks. Surrounded by palaces, ghats, temples, and historic architecture, the lake creates postcard-perfect views that have captivated travelers for generations.

03
Landmark 03

Jagdish Temple: A Masterpiece of Spiritual Architecture

Located just outside City Palace, Jagdish Temple is among the most visited religious landmarks in Udaipur. Built in 1651 by Maharana Jagat Singh I, the temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and is renowned for its detailed carvings and architectural craftsmanship. The towering structure rises above the surrounding streets, drawing visitors with its impressive design.

04
Landmark 04

Bagore Ki Haveli: Experience Rajasthan's Living Culture

Situated on the waterfront at Gangaur Ghat, Bagore Ki Haveli offers one of the most authentic cultural experiences in Udaipur. Originally built in the 18th century, the haveli has been carefully restored and converted into a museum showcasing royal artifacts, traditional costumes, folk art, and cultural displays. Many visitors come specifically for the evening cultural performances.

05
Landmark 05

Sajjangarh Monsoon Palace: The Best Sunset View in Udaipur

Perched high on a hilltop overlooking the city, Sajjangarh Monsoon Palace offers breathtaking panoramic views of Udaipur and its surrounding landscapes. Built by Maharana Sajjan Singh in the late 19th century, the palace was originally intended as an astronomical center and monsoon retreat. Today, it is one of the city's most popular viewpoints.

Practical Planning

How to Explore in 2 Days

This carefully planned itinerary helps you discover the best of the city without feeling rushed.

8:00 AM – Morning

City Palace

Begin your Udaipur journey at the magnificent City Palace. Overlooking Lake Pichola, this sprawling palace complex offers a fascinating introduction to the Mewar dynasty. Allow 2–3 hours.

  • Mor Chowk
  • Badi Mahal
  • Crystal Gallery
  • Palace Museum
  • Lake viewpoints

11:00 AM

Jagdish Temple

Just a few steps from City Palace stands the historic Jagdish Temple. Built in 1651, this intricately carved temple remains one of Udaipur's most important religious landmarks. Time required: 30–45 minutes.

  • Intricate stone carvings
  • Sculpted pillars
  • Daily religious ceremonies

12:00 PM – 1:30 PM

Lunch in Old City

Explore the historic lanes surrounding City Palace, then enjoy a relaxed lunch at a rooftop restaurant overlooking the lakes — one of the most memorable dining experiences in the city.

  • Handicraft shops
  • Miniature painting studios
  • Rooftop restaurants with lake views
  • Historic havelis

4:00 PM

Lake Pichola Boat Ride

No first trip to Udaipur is complete without experiencing Lake Pichola from the water. A boat ride offers unique views of the city's iconic skyline during the most beautiful light of the day.

  • City Palace views
  • Jag Mandir
  • Lake Palace
  • Historic ghats

6:00 PM – Sunset

Sunset at Gangaur Ghat

Gangaur Ghat is one of the most atmospheric places in Udaipur. As the sun sets behind the hills, locals gather along the waterfront while the lake reflects the city's historic skyline.

  • Sunset photography
  • Lakeside atmosphere
  • Bagore Ki Haveli evening show nearby

9:00 AM

Bagore Ki Haveli

Start your second morning at the historic Bagore Ki Haveli on Gangaur Ghat. Explore the museum's royal artifacts, traditional costumes, and cultural exhibits before the crowds arrive.

  • Royal artifacts collection
  • Traditional costumes
  • Folk art exhibitions
  • Lakefront setting

12:00 PM

Local Markets

Explore Udaipur's vibrant markets — a treasure trove of traditional craftsmanship. Markets around Hathipole and Bapu Bazaar are particularly popular among travelers.

  • Handmade textiles
  • Silver jewelry
  • Miniature paintings
  • Traditional handicrafts

3:00 PM

Fateh Sagar Lake

Compared to Lake Pichola, Fateh Sagar Lake offers a more relaxed, local atmosphere. The drive along the lake road is scenic and the Aravalli Hills provide a beautiful backdrop.

  • Lakeside promenade
  • Aravalli Hills backdrop
  • Local food stalls
  • Peaceful atmosphere

5:00 PM – Sunset

Sajjangarh Monsoon Palace

End your Udaipur journey at Sajjangarh Monsoon Palace. The views from here are unmatched — a 360-degree panorama of lakes, hills, and the royal skyline glowing beneath the setting sun.

  • Lake Pichola panorama
  • Fateh Sagar Lake view
  • Aravalli Hills vista
  • Udaipur city skyline
In-Depth Planning

The Perfect 2-Day Udaipur Itinerary

A detailed, hour-by-hour breakdown — so you never miss a moment of the City of Lakes.

Day 1: Discover Udaipur's Royal Heritage and Lakes

8:00
AM
City Palace

8:00 AM – Start with City Palace

Begin at the Magnificent City Palace

Allow 2–3 hours to explore fully

Begin your Udaipur journey at the magnificent City Palace. Overlooking Lake Pichola, this sprawling palace complex offers a fascinating introduction to the history of the Mewar dynasty. Wander through ornate courtyards, royal chambers, colorful mosaics, and museums that showcase centuries of royal heritage.

  • Mor Chowk — The legendary peacock courtyard
  • Badi Mahal — Garden palace with stunning city views
  • Crystal Gallery — Royal crystal artifacts collection
  • Palace Museum — Centuries of Mewar royal heritage
  • Lake viewpoints — Panoramic vistas over Lake Pichola

11:00 AM – Visit Jagdish Temple

A Spiritual Landmark of the Old City

Time required: 30–45 minutes

Just a few steps from City Palace stands the historic Jagdish Temple. Built in 1651, this intricately carved temple remains one of Udaipur's most important religious landmarks. Its impressive architecture and spiritual atmosphere make it worth a short stop.

  • Intricate stone carvings on the façade
  • Beautifully sculpted pillars and archways
  • Daily religious ceremonies throughout the morning

12:00 PM – Explore Udaipur's Old City

Wander the Historic Lanes

The narrow lanes surrounding City Palace offer a glimpse into the city's historic character. Unlike modern commercial districts, the Old City retains much of its original charm.

  • Traditional handicraft shops
  • Miniature painting studios
  • Local cafés and street food
  • Historic havelis and carved doorways
  • Colorful markets with local life

1:30 PM – Lunch with a View

Rooftop Dining Above the Lakes

Udaipur is known for its rooftop restaurants offering panoramic views of lakes and palaces. Enjoy a relaxed lunch while taking in views that make this one of the most memorable dining experiences in the city.

  • Lake Pichola views from the rooftop
  • Jag Mandir on the shimmering water
  • City Palace silhouette on the hill

4:00 PM – Boat Ride on Lake Pichola

The Essential Udaipur Experience

No first trip to Udaipur is complete without experiencing Lake Pichola from the water. A boat ride offers unique views of the city's iconic skyline. The lake's tranquil atmosphere provides a refreshing break from sightseeing.

  • City Palace reflected in the water
  • Jag Mandir island palace
  • Lake Palace hotel
  • Historic ghats along the shore

6:00 PM – Sunset at Gangaur Ghat

The City's Most Romantic Spot

Gangaur Ghat is one of the most atmospheric places in Udaipur. As the sun sets behind the hills, locals gather along the waterfront while the lake reflects the city's historic skyline. For photographers and couples, this is one of the most romantic spots in the city.

7:00 PM – Cultural Show at Bagore Ki Haveli

End Day 1 with Living Tradition

End your first day at Bagore Ki Haveli. Many travelers consider this the most authentic cultural experience in Udaipur — an immersive performance of Rajasthan's folk heritage.

  • Folk dances of Rajasthan
  • Puppet shows and storytelling
  • Traditional folk music
  • Rajasthan's rich artistic heritage

Day 2: Scenic Views, Local Experiences and Hidden Charm

8:00
AM
Fateh Sagar Lake

8:00 AM – Breakfast by Fateh Sagar Lake

A Peaceful Local Morning

Start your morning at Fateh Sagar Lake. Compared to Lake Pichola, it offers a more relaxed local atmosphere. Morning walks here provide beautiful views of the Aravalli Hills, calm lake waters, and everyday local life.

10:00 AM – Explore Saheliyon Ki Bari

The Royal Women's Garden

This elegant garden was built for royal women and remains one of Udaipur's most peaceful attractions. It's an ideal stop for travelers interested in Rajasthan's royal lifestyle.

  • Marble fountains
  • Lotus pools
  • Landscaped garden paths
  • Historic royal architecture

12:00 PM – Discover Local Markets

Traditional Crafts and Bazaars

Spend a few hours exploring Udaipur's vibrant markets. Markets around Hathipole and Bapu Bazaar are particularly popular for their authentic craftsmanship and variety.

  • Handmade textiles and fabrics
  • Silver jewelry and accessories
  • Leather goods
  • Miniature paintings
  • Traditional handicrafts

2:00 PM – Relax and Enjoy Local Cuisine

Authentic Rajasthani Flavours

Use the afternoon to sample traditional Rajasthani dishes. Food is an essential part of the Udaipur experience and a window into the region's heritage.

  • Dal Baati Churma — Rajasthan's signature dish
  • Gatte Ki Sabzi — gram flour curry
  • Ker Sangri — desert vegetable specialty
  • Laal Maas — rich spiced meat dish

4:30 PM – Visit Sajjangarh Monsoon Palace

The Grand Finale

Head towards Sajjangarh Monsoon Palace for the most spectacular views in the city. Perched high above Udaipur, the views here cannot be matched anywhere else in the city.

  • Lake Pichola in the distance
  • Fateh Sagar Lake panorama
  • Aravalli Hills stretching to the horizon
  • Udaipur city skyline at golden hour

6:30 PM – Sunset Over Udaipur

A Perfect Farewell

Watching the sunset from Monsoon Palace is the perfect way to conclude your Udaipur itinerary. As the city lights begin to appear below and the lakes glow beneath the evening sky, you'll understand why Udaipur is considered one of India's most beautiful cities.
